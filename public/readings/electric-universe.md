## The Signal and the Noise

Every second, your nervous system processes roughly 11 million bits of sensory data. You're conscious of maybe 50. The rest gets compressed, filtered, discarded by systems you never agreed to install. Evolution did the wiring. Culture did the tuning. And now algorithms do the pruning.

Most people walk around receiving a curated sliver of reality and calling it the whole picture. The tools they carry (phones, feeds, interfaces) narrow the aperture further. Not by accident. By design.

The electric universe is what happens when you widen it back.

Not metaphorical. Literal.

Your brain runs on electrical impulses. Synapses fire at measurable frequencies. Thoughts have waveforms. Emotions have signatures. When neuroscientists map the default mode network (the part of your brain that handles self-reflection and future planning), they're mapping an electrical system.

The tools we build at the Academy interact with that system. Morning Pages lower cortisol by redirecting the electrical chatter of anxiety into structured output. Artist Dates spike dopamine and serotonin by feeding the brain novel input it didn't predict. You're not journaling. You're rewiring.
